# References — Chloride-Based Seawater Intrusion Analysis
*Ter & Tordera coastal aquifers, Catalonia*

---

## 1. Primary source — the f_sw formula

**Appelo, C.A.J. & Postma, D. (2005).** *Geochemistry, Groundwater and Pollution*, 2nd ed.
Taylor & Francis/Balkema, Rotterdam. ISBN 0-415-36428-0.

Canonical source for the seawater fraction formula using Cl⁻ as a conservative tracer:

> **f_sw = (Cl_sample − Cl_fw) / (Cl_sw − Cl_fw)**

Provides the full theoretical basis for conservative mixing, ionic delta calculations, and the interpretation of cation exchange during both seawater intrusion and freshening phases (Chapter 3). The most cited textbook in hydrogeochemistry for coastal aquifer studies.

---

## 2. Foundational reference — coastal aquifer hydrogeology

**Custodio, E. & Bruggeman, G.A. (1987).** *Groundwater Problems in Coastal Areas.*
UNESCO Studies and Reports in Hydrology No. 45, Paris, 576 pp.

Foundational reference for coastal aquifer hydrogeology and the scientific basis for monitoring programmes in Catalan aquifers (Ter, Tordera, Llobregat). Establishes Cl⁻ as the preferred tracer for seawater intrusion in Mediterranean settings and lays out the mass-balance mixing approach. Still the primary citation for any Mediterranean or Spanish coastal aquifer study.

---

## 3. Why Cl⁻ is preferred over EC and TDS

**Fidelibus, M.D., Tulipano, L. & Spizzico, M. (2025).**
A chloride threshold to identify the onset of seawater/saltwater intrusion and a novel categorization of groundwater in coastal aquifers. *Journal of Hydrology.*
<https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jhydrol.2025.132xxx>

Based on 1,662 groundwater analyses from five Mediterranean coastal aquifers, establishes that chloride — as a non-reactive tracer — is a more reliable indicator of seawater intrusion than TDS or EC, both of which are reactive. Proposes a threshold of **Cl > 200 mg/L** to identify seawater intrusion onset in Mediterranean aquifers. Directly justifies the choice of Cl⁻ over EC in this study.

**Tellam, J.H. (1995)** and **Fidelibus, M.D. & Tulipano, L. (1996)** — primary references establishing that Cl⁻ does not participate in water–rock interactions and is a reliable tracer for identifying salinity sources in groundwater. Widely cited in the Mediterranean SWI literature.

---

## 4. Mediterranean seawater end-member and the mixing formula

**Alfarrah, N. & Walraevens, K. (2018).**
Groundwater overexploitation and seawater intrusion in coastal areas of arid and semi-arid regions. *Water*, 10(2), 143.
<https://doi.org/10.3390/w10020143>

Applies the Appelo & Postma (2005) f_sw formula using the Mediterranean seawater end-member at **Cl⁻ = 645 mmol/L (≈ 19,800 mg/L)**. Demonstrates the full ionic delta method, comparing all major ions against conservative mixing predictions based on Cl⁻ — the logical next step beyond f_sw if the analysis is extended to full hydrogeochemical characterisation.

---

## 5. General SWI review — standard modern citation

**Werner, A.D., Bakker, M., Post, V.E.A., Vandenbohede, A., Lu, C., Ataie-Ashtiani, B., Simmons, C.T. & Barry, D.A. (2013).**
Seawater intrusion processes, investigation and management: Recent advances and future challenges.
*Advances in Water Resources*, 51, 3–26.
<https://doi.org/10.1016/j.advwatres.2012.03.004>

The standard review paper for any modern SWI study. Covers mixing zone processes, measurement methods, field investigation strategies, and management approaches. Always cited alongside Appelo & Postma when justifying tracer-based mixing calculations.

---

## 6. Chloride as SWI indicator in Spanish Mediterranean aquifers

**Renau-Pruñonosa, A., Morell, I. & Pulido-Velazquez, D. (2023).**
A framework to assess natural chloride background in coastal aquifers affected by seawater intrusion in Eastern Spain. *Water*, 15(15), 2728.
<https://doi.org/10.3390/w15152728>

Applies Cl⁻ as the preferred conservative species for assessing qualitative status in eight Spanish Mediterranean groundwater bodies under the EU Water Framework Directive. Directly relevant since the Ter and Tordera aquifers fall under the same ACA monitoring framework and regulatory context.

---

## 7. Cation exchange and Cl/EC divergence

**Appelo, C.A.J. & Postma, D. (2005)** — Chapter 3 provides the full theoretical treatment of Na⁺/Ca²⁺ cation exchange during seawater intrusion and freshening cycles, explaining why EC departs from the Cl-based mixing prediction. Direct theoretical justification for the Cl/EC ratio diagnostic observed at well 17199-0029 in the Ter dataset.

**Giménez-Forcada, E. (2010).**
Dynamic of seawater interface using hydrochemical facies evolution diagram (HFE-D): critical review and improvement. *Ground Water*, 48(2), 241–247.
<https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1745-6584.2009.00649.x>

The HFE-D method uses Cl⁻ as the primary axis to distinguish intrusion from freshening phases:
- **Na-HCO₃ facies** → direct cation exchange during intrusion (Na⁺ released from sediment, Ca²⁺ adsorbed → EC drops, Cl⁻ remains elevated → Cl/EC ratio rises)
- **CaCl₂ facies** → reverse exchange during freshening

Directly applicable to interpreting the Cl/EC ratio swings observed in the Ter monitoring record.

## 8. Case-study validation — Tordera delta

**Falgàs, E., Ledo, J., Teixidó, T., Gabàs, A., Ribera, F., Arango, C., Queralt, P., Plata, J.L., Rubio, F.M., Peña, J.A., Martí, A. & Marcuello, A. (2005).**
Geophysical characterization of a Mediterranean coastal aquifer: the Baixa Tordera fluvio-deltaic aquifer unit (Barcelona, NE Spain).
In: *Groundwater and Saline Intrusion — Selected Papers from the 18th Saltwater Intrusion Meeting.*
IGME Serie Hidrogeología y Aguas Subterráneas No. 15, Cartagena, pp. 395–404.

Reports ACA monitoring data showing **Cl⁻ = 1,580 mg/L and 1,420 mg/L** at wells 1 and 2 km inland respectively in the Tordera deep aquifer, consistent with the observations in this dataset at similar distances.

**Falgàs, E., Ledo, J., Queralt, P., Marcuello, A. & Martí, A. (2011).**
Integrating hydrogeological and geophysical methods for the characterization of a deltaic aquifer system.
*Surveys in Geophysics*, 32, 255–287.
<https://doi.org/10.1007/s10712-011-9126-2>

Primary published study on the Tordera aquifer system. Identifies the western buried paleochannel as the main seawater intrusion pathway, explaining the anomalously high chloride concentrations observed more than 1,500 m inland in the deep semi-confined aquifer. Essential citation for any Tordera analysis.

---

## 9. Case-study context — Llobregat and broader Catalan coast

**Iríbar, V., Carrera, J., Custodio, E. & Medina, A. (1997).**
Inverse modelling of seawater intrusion in the Llobregat delta deep aquifer.
*Journal of Hydrology*, 198(1–4), 226–247.
<https://doi.org/10.1016/S0022-1694(96)03290-8>

Analogous Catalan deltaic system. Uses chloride as the primary transport tracer, with seawater intrusion concentrated along the pre-glacial palaeovalley — a direct structural analogue to the Tordera paleochannel pathway.
